Explore Shkodra

The Cultural Capital of Northern Albania

Shkodra, one of Albania's oldest and most historic cities, sits majestically between the Albanian Alps and the vast Lake Shkodra. With roots dating back to the 4th century BC, this city has been a crossroads of civilizations and cultures throughout its long history.

Known as the "Gateway to the Albanian Alps," Shkodra offers visitors a perfect blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and vibrant urban life. The city's architecture reflects its diverse past, with influences from Illyrian, Venetian, Ottoman, and communist eras.

Today, Shkodra is renowned for its intellectual tradition, lively arts scene, and as a base for exploring the spectacular surrounding nature. The city's pedestrian-friendly center is filled with cafes, restaurants, and cultural institutions that buzz with energy day and night.

Must-See Attractions

Rozafa Castle

This legendary fortress offers panoramic views of Shkodra, Lake Shkodra, and the surrounding mountains. The castle dates back to Illyrian times and is steeped in myth and history.

Lake Shkodra

The largest lake in Southern Europe, shared with Montenegro. Take a boat tour to explore its tranquil waters, rich birdlife, and picturesque fishing villages along the shore.

Historic Center

Wander through Shkodra's pedestrian streets lined with Ottoman-era buildings, Catholic churches, and the famous "Photo Gallery" documenting Albania's communist past.
